Assessment of Developmental Needs

The exercise is driven by your requirement to meet the organisation’s future needs in line with its strategic goals and business plan. It allows an organization to prioritise the multiple competing needs, and to decide on where to focus time, attention and invest the finite resources most effectively.

As a process we engage with your senior strategic teams before joint session's with business leaders for goal and gap discovery. Concurrent and ongoing assessment processes are required with the layers of employee force to gain a wholesome view of the developmental needs and road map.

Strategic Developmental Map

Strategic maps represent diagram/s of the relationships between key areas of the organization and the plan to collaborate to achieve strategic objectives.

Strategic Mapping lays out the big picture down to the key constituents of its implementation.

These may include targeted activities to build employee skills include continuous learning, structured on-the- job training programs, and periodic testing of employees to ensure they know what they are supposed to know. Strategic Maps are a deft combination of a longer term view to short term or quarterly activities which are monitored and evaluated for impact.

Change Communication Design & Development

This involves articulating the key objectives of change and answering critical questions related to the change.
It typically includes:

  • Identifying key change agents and facilitators across the hierarchy.
  • ICoaching change agents and facilitators on verbal and non-verbal communication.
  • ICoaching change agents and facilitators on communicating positivity around the change, and in dealing with negativity.
  • IDocumenting the change, its impact, and expected actions and behaviours.
  • I Providing schedules and check-lists to change leaders, change agents and facilitators to ensure a synchronous implementation.

Coaching Program Delivery

Involves coach-protégé session agreement, coverage, and impact.
Includes:

  • Liaising with line and functional managers to gain buy-in for the program, and seek support to ensure day-to-day business priorities are managed along with coaching sessions to a win-win.

Resource (Instructor) Management

Involves actively managing a network of instructors towards achieving the organisation’s training goals.
It typically includes:

  • Identifying prospective instructors, based on the learning inventory that needs to be delivered.
  • Interviewing and empanelling instructors
  • Familiarizing instructors with organization culture, needs, learner profiles and goals to ensure effective delivery
  • Coaching instructors for delivery success along defined career path
  • Conducting Train-the-Trainer sessions to bring a level of homogeneity in instruction

Communication & Change Management

Involves supporting enterprises with articulating change lucidly for every level within the organisation.

At the leadership levels, it involves:

  • Supporting leaders to model the right change behaviours, including verbal and non-verbal ones
  • Coaching leaders in exhibiting openness and inclusion through the process of communicating and implementing change

At the staff levels, it involves enabling employees by asking and answering the following questions via multiple modes of communication:

  • Specifically from what to what?
  • WIIFM: How does my work change as a result of this change?
  • Will it really make a difference to me, to teams, and to the business? Or is it a compliance-related change?
  • How will success be measured? How will I be rewarded or held accountable for my participation and support of the change initiative?
